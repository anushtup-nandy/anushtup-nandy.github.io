Featured Project

Bio-Inspired Undulatory Fin Propulsion

Developed a bio-inspired robotic fin that mimics the undulatory motion of aquatic creatures for efficient underwater propulsion. The project involved designing the mechanical structure, implementing control algorithms, and optimizing performance.

Key Contributions:

  • Rapid prototyped a propulsion mechanism operating Arduino, servomotors, a 3D-printed hull, & CNC-machined fin-ray
  • Data analysis & visualization, with Python and OpenCV, of wave data captured at 5610 fps by a PTC high-speed camera
  • Design calculations & analysis of propulsive force to be 0.16N using NI DAQ 9237 module and single-axis load cell

Professional Summary

I am a Mechanical Engineer with a specialization in Robotics, currently pursuing my MS at Columbia University. My research and projects focus on motion planning, reinforcement learning, controls, and bio-inspired robotics. I'm passionate about developing intelligent systems that can adapt and learn from their environments.

My approach combines rigorous engineering principles with creative problem-solving to develop innovative robotic solutions. I believe in the power of interdisciplinary collaboration and constantly seek to expand my knowledge at the intersection of mechanical engineering, computer science, and artificial intelligence.
